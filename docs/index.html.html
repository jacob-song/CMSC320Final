Avocado Prices Exploration¶

By Jacob Song

Introduction¶

So what's the deal with Avocados?

They're savory, buttery, rich and creamy, something you would never expect from ordinary, most-of-the-time sweet fruits. With the health benefits of avocados, the ever-rising demands of "avocado toasts" and as the main ingredient of guacamole, avocados warrant their popularity of demands and their extragavent-than-average prices.

However, their prices have been fluctuating a lot in the recent years, and have hiked nearly double their average price during the year of 2017.

So can Exploratory Data Analysis tell us something about the price fluctuation of avocados?

Conclusion¶

So what is causing these price increase?

There are multiple causes! (source: https://www.cheatsheet.com/culture/why-avocados-are-so-expensive.hyml/)

  • The season: growing season is peak February and September, which means other months the harvest is less
  • Excessive heat: Extreme heatwaves in 2017 made avocados scarce
  • Drought: Excessive drought in California 2017 caused less harvest
  • Fire: Wildfire of California burned severe damage to avocado trees
  • Supply and demand: Ever increasing demand of Avocado (see Avocado Toast)

So it seems that the data was pointing us to the right direction, that volume, date and average price are somewhat correlated, and California as one of the main exporter of avocados and onen of the highest volume of avocados purchased, we can see that the supply dip in the month of October and September do indeed play the role in the average price fluctuations. And the listed factors for 2017 make sense to why avocados' average price was highest it has ever been in this dataset due to lack of supply to the vast demand.
